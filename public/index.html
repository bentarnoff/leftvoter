Florida

Florida has a closed primary. You must register as a Democrat by the deadline of August 1, 2016 to vote in the Democratic primary on July 16, 2016.

Tim Canova



"For the past four decades, the rules have been rigged in favor of Wall Street and the billionaire class."



Canova is a law professor and former Congressional aide who's written extensively on the Federal Reserve. He's running for a seat in Florida's 23rd Congressional District, which includes Weston, Hollywood, and Miami Beach. He's been endorsed by the Communications Workers of America, National Nurses United, Veterans for Bernie, and the Progressive Democrats of America.

Vote

  • Florida's Congressional Primaries are on July 16, 2016. You must be a registered Democrat to vote in the Democratic primary. All registered voters can vote in the Congressional general election on November 8, 2015.

Volunteer & Donate

  • Sign up for Canova's mailing list at his site.
  • Contribute to Canova's campaign through ActBlue.

Read

  • Read about Canova in The New Republic, The Intercept, and his Reddit AMA.

Washington

Washington has a top-two primary, which means that voters vote for both parties' candidates all at once. The two candidates with the most votes advance to the general election. To vote in the primary on August 2, 2016, you must register by the deadline of July 4 (online or mail-in) or July 25 (in-person). To vote in the Congressional general election on November 8, 2016, you must register by the deadline of October 10 (online or mail-in) or October 31 (in-person).

Pramila Jayapal



"The corporate and special interests have their voice in Congress, and they have too many members scared of their power."



Jayapal is a Democratic Washington State Senator and an ally of socialist Seattle City Councilwoman Kshama Sawant. She's running for a seat in Washington's 7th Congressional District, which includes Vashon Island, Edmonds, Shoreline, Kenmore, and most of Seattle. She's been endorsed by Bernie Sanders, Bill McKibben, United Food and Commercial Workers International Union, United Auto Workers Union, and American Federation of Government Employees. Visit her campaign site.

Vote

  • Washington's Congressional Primaries are on August 2, 2016. All registered voters can vote in the primary and in the Congressional general election on November 8, 2015.
